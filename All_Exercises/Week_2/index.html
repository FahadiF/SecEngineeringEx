1. What does the "Not Secure" warning mean in the first picture and what risks does visiting sites with the warning pose?

The "Not Secure" warning on the first site means the website is using HTTP (HyperText Transfer Protocol) instead of HTTPS (HyperText Transfer Protocol Secure). This means that the connection between the user and the website is not encrypted.

Visting sites like this involve a risk of man-in-the-middle attacks where hackers can intercept and manipulate the data we're sending. If we're entering sensitive information like passwords or credit card details, it could be easily stolen.
